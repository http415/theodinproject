Takeout style broccoli with garlic sauce

Takeout-style broccoli with garlic sauce is a great dish to have with any meal. Served with some white or brown rice, it’s tasty and guilt-free, and it’s easier than you’d think to emulate that restaurant flavor!

Ingredients

  • Broccoli
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Soy sauce
  • Rice wine vinegar
  • Sugar
  • Chicken or vegetable stock
  • Sesame oil
  • Ground white pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • Minced garlic
  • Shoaxing wine
  • Cornstarch
  • Homemade chili oil

Steps

  1. Wash the broccoli, mix the soy sauces, rice wine vinegar, sugar, stock, sesame oil, and ground white pepper then set aside
  2. Heat the wok over high heat. Ad the vegetable oil, garlic, and broccoli. Quickly stir-fry so the garlic doesn't burn
  3. Stir fry for 15 seconds, and the shaoxing rice wine around the perimeter of the wok. Give everything a quick stir fry and add in the sauce mixture. Let the sauce come to a boil, mix the cornstarch and water together. Turn the heat down slightly and cook the broccoli a bit longer before adding the cornstarch slurry if you like your broccoli softer
  4. Add the cornstarch while stirring the broccoli and sauce constantly. Let the sauce cool and thicken until it coats the back of a spoon. If you like a thick sauce, and more cornstarch and water, if you like it thinner, add less. Stir in the optional chili oil at this point and serve!
